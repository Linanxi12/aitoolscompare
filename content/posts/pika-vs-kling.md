## 3 Key Tests 🔬

### Test 1: Video Quality

**Prompt:** A person walking through a rainy city street. **Kling:** Better human motion (Kuaishou's video dataset advantage), natural gait, consistent rain physics, good atmosphere. 2-min max length. **Pika:** Good quality but simpler — less natural motion, more "AI video" feel, shorter clips (10-15 seconds). Pika's quality is optimized for quick consumption formats.

<div class="verdict-box"><div class="verdict-label">📝 Verdict</div><p class="verdict-text"><strong>Kling — better quality, especially human motion and longer clips.</strong></p></div>

### Test 2: Social Media Workflow

**Task:** Create 5 TikTok-ready videos with trending effects. **Pika:** Pikaffects (explode, melt, morph) are fun and social-native. Fast generation (~30-60 sec). Short clip format matches social media consumption. The creative effects are Pika's differentiator. **Kling:** Better quality, no creative effects. Pika's effects-first approach makes it the more fun tool for social content.

<div class="verdict-box"><div class="verdict-label">📝 Verdict</div><p class="verdict-text"><strong>Pika — built for social media; effects are unique and engaging.</strong></p></div>

### Test 3: Free Tier and Value

**Pika:** Free tier with limited generations; $10/month Standard, $35/month Unlimited. **Kling:** Generous daily free credits; competitively priced paid plans. Kling's free tier is more sustainable for ongoing experimentation. Pika's $10/month entry is the most affordable paid plan in AI video.

<div class="verdict-box"><div class="verdict-label">📝 Verdict</div><p class="verdict-text"><strong>Kling for free tier; Pika for cheapest paid plan.</strong></p></div>
