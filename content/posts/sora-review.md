### Scenario 1: Creative Video Quality

**Test method:** Generate 10 clips across diverse scenarios — nature landscapes, urban scenes, product visualization, character animation, abstract/artistic, historical recreation, food preparation, sports action, underwater, and sci-fi.

Sora's creative ceiling is genuinely impressive. The underwater scene — bioluminescent creatures drifting through a coral reef — looked like a nature documentary. The historical recreation — a 1920s New York street scene — had atmospheric authenticity that competitors struggle to match. The physics simulation sets Sora apart: objects have weight, water flows naturally, fabric drapes correctly, reflections behave realistically.

Where it stumbles: character faces at medium distance can blur or morph, fast action (sports) shows temporal artifacts, and complex multi-subject scenes sometimes lose track of secondary elements.

### Scenario 2: Prompt Adherence

**Test method:** 15 prompts ranging from simple ("a cat sleeping on a windowsill, golden hour") to complex ("a chef in a busy restaurant kitchen, tracking shot following a dish from stove to table, steam rising, warm lighting, shallow depth of field, 24fps cinematic").

Sora handles simple to moderate prompts reliably — the cat, the landscape, the product shot all matched expectations well. On complex prompts, adherence degrades. The chef tracking shot was beautiful but missed the "shallow depth of field" instruction and the steam was inconsistent. A prompt for "three specific colored balloons" generated four balloons with wrong colors.

Prompt adherence is better than early text-to-image models at equivalent maturity, but not yet at the "what you write is what you get" reliability that production work requires. Storyboard-style prompting (describing frame by frame) works better than single-paragraph descriptions.

### Scenario 3: Practical Production Workflow

**Test method:** Try to create a 30-second social media ad — from concept to final export — using only Sora.

This is where Sora shows its biggest weakness. Generation takes 1-5 minutes per clip. There's no inpainting to fix small errors (must regenerate entirely). No motion tracking for text overlays. No timeline for arranging multiple clips. The workflow is: generate → download → edit in external software → re-generate if something's wrong → download again → edit again.

Compare to Runway Gen-4, where you can generate, inpaint errors, adjust motion, add audio, and export — all in-browser. Sora generates better individual clips. Runway delivers a better end-to-end production experience.

## What Makes Sora Unique

### Physics Simulation
Sora's most impressive technical achievement is its world-model-like understanding of physics. Objects obey gravity. Water flows downhill. Characters occlude correctly when passing behind objects. Fabric drapes and folds naturally. This isn't perfect — liquids sometimes defy physics, and fast motion causes artifacts — but it's a clear generation ahead of any competitor. Runway and Pika videos look like generated images in motion; Sora videos look like a world being observed.

### 60-Second Generation
Most competitors cap at 10-30 seconds. Sora generates up to 60 seconds in a single clip. For narrative storytelling, establishing shots, and atmospheric sequences, this length difference matters — you can build a scene rather than just a moment.

### Resolution & Aspect Ratios
Sora supports up to 1080p at launch with widescreen (16:9), vertical (9:16), and square (1:1) aspect ratios. The vertical support makes it viable for TikTok/Reels/Shorts content, which is where most AI video ends up. 4K is not yet available — a notable gap for professional work — though OpenAI has indicated it's on the roadmap.

### Integration with ChatGPT
Sora lives inside ChatGPT rather than as a standalone app. This means you can iterate on prompts conversationally ("make it moodier," "slow down the camera movement"), which is more natural than the prompt-box-and-wait interface of standalone tools. The trade-off: no standalone app means no dedicated video editing interface.

## Content Policy and Restrictions

OpenAI has implemented aggressive safety measures on Sora: no public figures, no realistic violence, no explicit content, watermarking on all outputs, and C2PA metadata embedding. These are important ethical safeguards — Sora is the most responsible launch in the category. They also mean some creative use cases (action scenes, political satire, documentary recreation) are restricted in ways they aren't on less-moderated competitors. For compliance-conscious creators, Sora's safety infrastructure is a feature. For unrestricted creative exploration, it's a limitation.
