### Scenario 1: Photorealism & Image Quality

**Test method:** Generate real-world prompts — "a weathered fisherman at golden hour, editorial photography, 85mm f/1.4" — and rate against professional photography standards.

Midjourney v7 produces images so close to real photography that non-experts can't tell the difference. Skin texture, fabric weave, lighting falloff, depth of field — all rendered at a level that would pass for a professional photoshoot. In blind comparisons with Flux (8.6), Midjourney still leads on organic subject matter (people, nature, food) but the gap has narrowed on landscapes and product photography.

<div class="verdict-box">
  <div class="verdict-label">📝 Verdict</div>
  <p class="verdict-text">
    <strong>9.4/10 — the photorealism gold standard.</strong> Not untouchable anymore — Flux is at 9.0 — but still the reference every other tool is measured against.
  </p>
</div>

### Scenario 2: Prompt Adherence

**Test method:** "A wooden bowl with exactly 4 wine glasses, 3 lit candles, and 2 open books, 45° angle." Test counting accuracy and compositional precision.

This is Midjourney's weakest dimension. The 4 glasses might be 3, the 3 candles might be 2 or 4, and the 45° angle becomes "somewhere around 45°." For creative work where interpretation is a feature, this is fine. For client work requiring precise specs, this is the reason to use DALL-E 3 (9.2 prompt adherence).

Text rendering remains a known weakness — logos, signs, and posters with readable text still come out garbled or mispelled. Midjourney has improved (`--sref` style references help) but hasn't solved this.

<div class="verdict-box">
  <div class="verdict-label">📝 Verdict</div>
  <p class="verdict-text">
    <strong>7.5/10 — functional but the weakest dimension.</strong> Midjourney makes beautiful images. It does not make precisely what you asked for. For that, use DALL-E 3 or Flux.
  </p>
</div>

### Scenario 3: Artistic Style & Creativity

**Test method:** Generate across three wildly different styles — "cyberpunk samurai in ukiyo-e woodblock," "Art Deco travel poster for Mars," "children's book watercolor of a robot gardening."

This is Midjourney's superpower. Every style prompt produces convincing, aesthetically coherent output. The ukiyo-e piece looked like an authentic 19th-century print. The Art Deco Mars poster could be a museum piece. The watercolor robot had genuine brush-texture authenticity.

No other tool matches Midjourney's style range out of the box. Flux and SD3 can match or exceed it with specific LoRAs and fine-tuning, but that takes time. Midjourney gives it to you in one prompt.
